Historical Timeline

Key events in Philippine history from pre-colonial times to the modern era

1521

Ferdinand Magellan's Arrival

Portuguese explorer Ferdinand Magellan arrives in the Philippines, claiming the islands for Spain and beginning the Spanish colonial period.

1565

Spanish Colonization Begins

Miguel López de Legazpi establishes the first permanent Spanish settlement in Cebu, marking the beginning of 333 years of Spanish rule.

1896

Philippine Revolution

The Cry of Pugad Lawin sparks the Philippine Revolution against Spanish rule, led by figures like José Rizal, Andrés Bonifacio, and Emilio Aguinaldo.

1898

Declaration of Independence

Emilio Aguinaldo declares Philippine independence from Spain on June 12, though this leads to the Philippine-American War.

1946

Independence from the United States

The Philippines gains full independence on July 4, becoming the first Asian country to achieve independence from Western colonial powers.

Key Historical Figures

Important individuals who shaped Philippine history

José Rizal

National Hero

Writer, physician, and revolutionary whose novels "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo" exposed Spanish colonial abuses and inspired the Philippine Revolution.

1861-1896

Andrés Bonifacio

Revolutionary Leader

Founder of the Katipunan secret society and leader of the 1896 Philippine Revolution. Known as the "Father of the Philippine Revolution."

1863-1897

Emilio Aguinaldo

First President

Military leader who became the first President of the Philippines. Led the fight against both Spanish and American forces.

1869-1964

Apolinario Mabini

Prime Minister

Brilliant paralytic known as the "Sublime Paralytic" and "Brains of the Revolution." Served as Prime Minister of the First Philippine Republic.

1864-1903

Historical Glossary

Key terms and concepts in Philippine history

Encomienda System

A Spanish colonial system where land and native inhabitants were granted to Spanish encomenderos who were responsible for their conversion to Christianity and protection.

Galleon Trade

The trans-Pacific trade route established by Spain connecting Manila to Acapulco, Mexico, bringing silver from the Americas and goods from Asia.

Ilustrados

Educated Filipinos who studied in Europe during the Spanish colonial period and became advocates for reform and eventual independence.

Katipunan

A secret revolutionary society founded by Andrés Bonifacio in 1892 that organized the Philippine Revolution against Spanish rule.

Propaganda Movement

A reform movement led by Filipino intellectuals in the late 19th century that sought peaceful change through education and legal means.

Reduccion

The Spanish policy of concentrating indigenous populations into centralized settlements called reducciones for easier administration and Christianization.
