Did You Know?

José Rizal was a true polymath, speaking 22 languages fluently and earning degrees in medicine, philosophy, and letters across Europe
The Cavite Mutiny of 1872 sparked by just 200 soldiers shook Spanish colonial rule and led to executions that ignited the revolution
Emilio Aguinaldo declared Philippine independence on June 12, 1898, establishing Asia's first democratic republic
Andrés Bonifacio, a humble typesetter, founded the secret Katipunan society that became a revolutionary force
The Philippine Revolution (1896-1898) lasted only 2 years but fundamentally transformed Asian history forever
Apolinario Mabini, paralyzed yet unbreakable, served as Prime Minister and architect of independence while confined to a wheelchair
Gregorio del Pilar, the 'Boy General,' heroically died at just 26 years old defending the Republic at Tirad Pass
José Rizal's groundbreaking novel Noli Me Tángere was so revolutionary that Spanish authorities banned it immediately upon publication
Rizal's final masterpiece Mi Último Adiós, written the night before his execution, remains one of literature's most powerful farewell poems
